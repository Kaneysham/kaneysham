The Aurora Borealis

Where Are They Visible?

The Aurora Borealis are typically visible in places with cooler climates such as Alaska, Canada, Iceland, Greenland, etc.

How is the Aurora Borealis Created?

They are caused by the sun. When a solar storm approaches us, the energy can travel down the earths magnetic field lines at the nroth & south pole.Then the energy interacts with the gases in our atmosphere creating these displays in the sky.

Is the Aurora Rare?

They're rare in places with lower latitudes, however places close to the polar circles see these displays often.

How Long Do These Displays Last?

These displays can last a few minutes to a few hours!

Whens the Best Time to See the Lights?

The best time for these lights is October and April.

Fun Facts

  • The term aurora borealis was made in 1619 .
  • Earth isnt the only planet with auroras, other planets do too.
  • The oldest record of an aurora dates back to 2600 BC
